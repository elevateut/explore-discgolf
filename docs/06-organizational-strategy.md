# Organizational Strategy

## Use the existing 501(c)(3) — don't start from scratch

Given ElevateUT's existing nonprofit infrastructure, the strongest path is **not** creating a separate brand-new entity. The cleaner model is to run this as a **dedicated program or campaign inside the existing 501(c)(3)**, with a public-facing name and office-by-office toolkit.

**Why a 501(c)(3) is essential:**
- BLM cooperating associations must maintain 501(c)(3) status per BLM Handbook H-8362-1; loss of status terminates agreements
- Nearly all outdoor recreation grants (REI, NFWF, Conservation Alliance, Patagonia, LWCF pass-through) require 501(c)(3) status
- Tax-deductible donations are critical for building a membership base
- Under the IRS expenditure test (Form 5768), a 501(c)(3) under $500K budget can spend up to **20% on lobbying** — more than sufficient

**Only add a separate 501(c)(4) if heavy lobbying becomes central.** IRS says a 501(c)(4) social welfare organization may lobby as its primary activity. That is useful only if legislative advocacy becomes a major long-term lane. Most groups should not start there.

### If starting a new entity is required

**Fiscal sponsorship bridge (months 1-12):** Partner with the Community Foundation of Utah or Tides Center (7-9% fee) for immediate fundraising and grant eligibility while IRS processes Form 1023 (3-6 months). You have 27 months from incorporation to file and receive retroactive tax-exempt status.

| Step | Timeline | Cost |
|------|----------|------|
| Utah state incorporation | 1-5 business days | ~$70 |
| EIN via IRS.gov | Same day | Free |
| IRS Form 1023 filing | 3-6 months processing | $600 |
| Attorney/consultant review | 2-4 weeks prep | $500-$1,500 |
| **Total** | **4-8 months** | **$1,170-$2,170** |

## Operating model: hub-and-spoke campaign

- **Central hub** that translates the statute, builds packet templates, tracks evidence, and manages the public story
- **Local ambassadors**, one per BLM office or district, who tailor packets and maintain relationships
- **Technical advisory bench** for accessibility, course design, stewardship, risk, and land-use compatibility
- **Partner layer** including counties, chambers, adaptive-sports groups, youth providers, veterans groups, and local clubs

This mirrors the law's repeated emphasis on stakeholder consultation, local coordination, and leverage of non-federal resources.

## Public-facing initiative name

Suggested names: **Public Lands Disc Golf Alliance (PLDGA)** | American Disc Golf Conservation (ADGC) | Disc Golf Access Alliance | National Disc Golf Lands Trust | Chains on Public Lands

Branding direction: pair credibility-signaling language ("Alliance," "Conservation," "Access") with disc golf identity. Avoid names confusable with PDGA. Use imagery combining iconic western landscapes (red rock, sagebrush, ponderosa) with disc golf baskets.

## Mission, vision, and programs

**Mission:** To expand and protect disc golf access on America's public lands through federal partnerships, community-driven course development, environmental stewardship, and policy advocacy.

**Vision:** Every American lives within reach of a free disc golf course on well-managed public land.

**Five core program areas:**

1. **Federal Lands Partnerships** — Build cooperative agreements with BLM field offices; develop MOUs with USFS ranger districts; serve as the recognized national partner for disc golf on federal land
2. **Course Development** — Identify, propose, fund, design, and volunteer-build disc golf courses on BLM and USFS land using EXPLORE Act authorities (Sections 341, 351, 153)
3. **Policy & Advocacy** — Participate in FICOR, NEPA comment processes, RMP revisions, and legislative development; join Outdoor Alliance and ORR; advocate for disc golf inclusion in recreation inventories (Section 112)
4. **Stewardship & Conservation** — Create an "Adopt-a-Course" federal lands stewardship program; develop environmental best practices for BLM terrain types; align with Leave No Trace
5. **Accessibility, Youth & Veterans** — Leverage Title II provisions for adaptive disc golf, youth programming, and veteran wellness partnerships

## Funding strategy

### Founding phase ($0-$50K)
- Founder personal investment + friends/family ($5K-$15K)
- Disc golf industry founding sponsors: Innova, Discraft, Dynamic Discs, MVP, Latitude 64 ($2,500-$10,000 each)
- Together Outdoors Pilot Grants (ORR program for orgs under $300K revenue with <$100K prior grants; fiscal sponsorship eligible)
- Patagonia Retail Grants (Salt Lake City store; $5K-$15K; deadlines May 31 and September 30; fiscal sponsorships accepted)

### Growth phase ($50K-$300K)
- **REI Cooperative Action Fund** — $15K-$100K+ for outdoor access and health equity; Spring/Fall cycles; requires 501(c)(3)
- **Conservation Alliance** — $34.9M distributed since 1989; 2026 Member Collective Grant Cycle inquiry period March 2-April 10, 2026; applications April 27-May 15
- **NFWF Five Star Grant Program** — community conservation grants
- **Land and Water Conservation Fund State Assistance** — 50/50 match through Utah Division of Outdoor Recreation; disc golf qualifies as "development of facilities for public outdoor recreation"
- **BLM Challenge Cost Share (CFDA 15.225)** — $500-$250,000 (average ~$25K); coordinate with BLM state/district offices
- **BLM Recreation Resource Management (CFDA 15.225)** — cooperative agreements for recreation facility development

### Institutional phase ($300K+)
- National membership program
- Industry partnership tiers ($5K-$50K annual)
- Federal cooperative agreement revenue
- Foundation grants (Walton Family Foundation, Wilburforce Foundation, Hewlett Foundation)

## Affiliate model (not competition)

Existing state-level disc golf nonprofits become **affiliates** — maintaining their own 501(c)(3) status, governance, and local programs while aligning on federal advocacy. The national org provides federal advocacy infrastructure, BLM engagement training, insurance access, shared CRM, and co-branding rights.

- **Affiliate** — Independent org, signs MOU, co-brands on federal advocacy, receives BLM toolkit
- **Chapter** — Operates under national's group tax exemption; for states without existing nonprofits
- Revenue sharing: 60% local, 40% national

**Affiliation requirements:**
1. Incorporated as a nonprofit (501(c)(3) preferred; state-level incorporation minimum)
2. Active disc golf programming
3. Signed MOU with national organization
4. Annual report on activities and BLM engagement
5. Alignment with Leave No Trace and stewardship principles

**MOU summary terms:**
- Term: 3 years, renewable
- Each party retains independent governance and finances
- National handles federal advocacy; affiliate handles local implementation
- Brand co-use with "Affiliated Chapter" designation
- Neither party may bind the other financially
- Dispute resolution: good-faith discussion, mediation, termination with 90-day notice
