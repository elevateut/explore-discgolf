State recreation grants are usually the **largest single line** in the funding stack for a BLM disc golf course. This guide is the field-ready summary; the full state-by-state inventory with agency URLs, cycle dates, and program-level detail lives in the project research at `docs/12-state-grant-funding.md`.

## The funding stack

The EXPLORE Act does **not** appropriate dedicated dollars for disc golf. A real BLM course gets paid for by stacking:

1. **Federal share via BLM** — Challenge Cost Share (CFDA 15.225, $500–$250K, average ~$25K) or Recreation Resource Management cooperative agreements
2. **State recreation grant** — the focus of this guide
3. **Non-federal match** — volunteer hours valued at DOL rates (Section 341 makes this viable without volunteer insurance), donated materials and pro services, cash, foundation grants
4. **Private/foundation layer** — REI Cooperative Action Fund, NFWF Five Star, Conservation Alliance, Patagonia, Rasmuson (AK), local community foundations

See the [Full Project Proposal Template](/resources/proposal-template) for the line items, and the [Making the Case](/resources/making-the-case) page for cost evidence.

## The two anchors

Two state programs stand clearly above the rest for a disc golf nonprofit:

- **Utah Outdoor Recreation Grant (UORG)** — explicitly lists "disc golf" as an eligible activity. Has funded "The Arena" in West Jordan ($200K, partnered with ElevateUT), Logan Outdoor Recreation Complex ($750K), and others. Up to **$1M** per project, ~50% match, **directly nonprofit-eligible**, $16M annual program.
- **New Mexico Trails+ Grant** — has awarded **$870K+** to three disc golf projects since FY2024 (City of Albuquerque, Glorieta Adventure Camps, Jemez Enterprises). Up to **$500K** for nonprofits. Recurring funding via the Land of Enchantment Legacy Fund. BLM-land precedent (Fort Craig, $80,650). FY2027 cycle opens July 1, 2026.

## The cross-cutting program: RTP

The Recreational Trails Program is the one federal pass-through that consistently allows direct nonprofit applicants in nearly every BLM state. ~$84M nationally, 20% match (volunteer labor accepted), state-administered.

**⚠️ Reauthorization risk:** RTP authorization expires after federal FY2026. File RTP applications promptly; treat them as both a primary funding source and a match-stacking tool for larger state programs.

## State-by-state quick reference

**Utah — gold standard.** UORG, CPR, RRI, RTP all directly nonprofit-eligible. Disc golf explicitly named.

**New Mexico — proven funding.** Trails+ has the deepest disc golf track record of any state grant program in the country.

**Colorado — large but indirect.** GOCO is huge ($1.4B since 1992) but requires a local-government partner. Cultivate municipal/county relationships first.

**Washington — deep portfolio.** RCO administers WWRP, YAF, PRA, NOVA, RTP. YAF is the highest-ceiling nonprofit-eligible grant in any BLM state.

**Oregon — strong programs, narrow nonprofit access.** LGGP requires local government; RTP is the directly-eligible alternative. Stewart Pond DGC near Roseburg is the BLM-land precedent.

**California — massive bond funding.** Prop 4 (2024) added $700M for parks. SPP Round 5 opens Summer/Fall 2026; nonprofits eligible; restricted to underserved communities.

**Nevada — Conserve Nevada is the front door.** Voter-renewed bond ($217.5M over 10 years). Ward Mountain DGC near Ely is the BLM-land precedent. SNPLMA (BLM-funded) requires a local government partner.

**Montana — lowest match in the country.** TSP requires only 10% match and stacks with RTP for a combined $200K with very low partner cost. Sheep Mountain near Clancy is the active BLM precedent (seeking formal designation).

**Idaho — RTP is the only realistic state path.** Most other IDPR programs are restricted to government entities or specific motorized uses. IDPR officially lists disc golf as a recognized park activity.

**Arizona — Heritage Fund recovering.** $1M FY2026 appropriation; HCR2011 ballot measure could add $10M annual lottery allocation in 2026. RTP ($1.82M annual) is the directly nonprofit-eligible workhorse.

**Wyoming — mineral wealth, modest recreation programs.** Outdoor Rec Collaborative Grant has funded BLM projects (Bighorn River, Bent Sprockets). BRC ($5M) requires local government.

**Alaska — foundations fill the state-grant gap.** RTP is directly nonprofit-eligible. Rasmuson Foundation and Anchorage Park Foundation are the dominant funders; Anchorage Disc Golf Association received a 2024 grant.

## Strategic sequencing for a national disc golf 501(c)(3)

1. **Secure BLM land authorization first.** State grants need a defensible site and a willing land manager before they will score well.
2. **File with directly nonprofit-eligible programs immediately.** UORG, Trails+, Conserve Nevada, RTPs in UT, OR, ID, AZ, AK, MT, WA. No political waiting.
3. **Cultivate local government partnerships in parallel** to unlock GOCO, WWRP, LGGP, LWCF, SNPLMA, Wyoming BRC.
4. **Stack foundation grants and small RTP awards as match** for larger asks. The Montana TSP+RTP pattern is the cleanest template.
5. **Build a cycle calendar** by January each year — most programs run on annual or biennial windows that don't overlap.

## Verify before you file

Every program-level claim — funding amount, eligibility, deadline, disc golf precedent — should be independently verified against the agency's current website before being used in a formal application. Cycle dates, match percentages, and 501(c)(3) eligibility rules change between years. The full inventory in `docs/12-state-grant-funding.md` includes the agency URLs.
