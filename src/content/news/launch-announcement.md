Today we're launching **EXPLORE Disc Golf** — an open source project by [ElevateUT](https://elevateut.org) (501(c)(3)) to expand disc golf on Bureau of Land Management public lands using authorities created by the EXPLORE Act (P.L. 118-234).

## The opportunity

The EXPLORE Act, signed into law on January 4, 2025, gives BLM new tools for recreation development: mandatory recreation inventories, expanded volunteer authority, Good Neighbor agreements for infrastructure construction, and streamlined permitting. These tools fit disc golf unusually well — but no disc golf organization has built the infrastructure to use them.

Meanwhile, disc golf has 11,165 courses nationally and 21.2 million annual rounds. Yet across BLM's 245 million surface acres, only three confirmed disc golf courses exist.

## What we're building

EXPLORE Disc Golf provides:

- **Education** on the EXPLORE Act and how its provisions apply to disc golf
- **A BLM office finder** to connect advocates with their local field offices
- **Engagement resources** including one-pagers, proposal templates, and talking points
- **Case studies** of existing BLM disc golf courses
- **AI-powered engagement packets** tailored to specific BLM offices (coming soon)

Everything is open source under the Apache-2.0 license. We believe the tools for public lands advocacy should be as accessible as the public lands themselves.
