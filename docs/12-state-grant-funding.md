# State Recreation Grant Programs

State-by-state inventory of recreation grant programs across the 12 BLM states, scored for a 501(c)(3) building disc golf courses on BLM-administered public land. This document is the canonical funding reference — the founding-phase numbers in [06-organizational-strategy.md](06-organizational-strategy.md) and the "federal share" line in [07-blm-engagement-package.md](07-blm-engagement-package.md) both depend on it.

## TL;DR

- **Every BLM state has a viable pathway**, but the landscape is uneven. The program-density spread between Utah and Alaska is roughly 6-to-1.
- **Two anchors stand above the rest.** Utah's UORG explicitly lists "disc golf" as an eligible activity and has funded multiple courses; New Mexico's Trails+ has awarded $870K+ to three disc golf projects since FY2024.
- **The Recreational Trails Program (RTP)** is the single most consistently nonprofit-eligible program nationally. It is also the most politically fragile — authorization expires after federal FY2026 and depends on the next surface transportation bill.
- **Roughly 60+ distinct programs** exist across these 12 states. Most require local government partnership; the ranked Top 15 below filters for the ones a national disc golf 501(c)(3) can actually use.
- **Strategic sequencing matters.** Secure BLM land authorization first → file with directly-eligible state programs → cultivate municipal/county partners for the larger gov-only programs → use foundation grants and small RTP awards as match for bigger asks.

## How disc golf gets paid for under the EXPLORE Act

The EXPLORE Act does not appropriate dedicated dollars for disc golf or for any specific recreation activity. The funding stack a partnership actually assembles looks like:

1. **Federal share via BLM** — Challenge Cost Share (CFDA 15.225, $500–$250K, average ~$25K) or Recreation Resource Management cooperative agreements. See [06-organizational-strategy.md](06-organizational-strategy.md#funding-strategy).
2. **State recreation grants** — the subject of this document. Several allow direct nonprofit applications; many require a local-government partner.
3. **Non-federal match** — volunteer hours valued at DOL rates (Section 341 makes this viable without insurance), donated materials, donated professional services, cash, and foundation/industry grants. See [04-making-the-case.md](04-making-the-case.md) for the cost evidence.
4. **Private/foundation layer** — REI Cooperative Action Fund, NFWF Five Star, Conservation Alliance, Patagonia, Rasmuson (AK), community foundations.

State grants are usually the **largest single line** in the stack. A national 501(c)(3) that can apply directly (rather than through a city or county) can move much faster.

## State-by-state inventory

### UTAH — the gold standard

Utah's Division of Outdoor Recreation, inside DNR, runs the most disc-golf-friendly grant portfolio of any BLM state.

**Utah Outdoor Recreation Grant (UORG)**
- Agency: Utah Division of Outdoor Recreation
- Funding source: state general fund legislative appropriation
- Range: Mini-Grant up to **$30,000**; Tier 1 $30,001–$500,000; Regional Asset Tier $500,001–**$1,000,000**
- Match: ~50% (in-kind labor/materials accepted)
- 501(c)(3): **directly eligible** alongside municipalities, state/federal agencies, tribes, public educational institutions
- Annual program: **$16M** distributed in 2025 across 90 projects; **$83M** since 2015
- Cycle: annual, January–March. 2026 cycle closed March 13; next cycle expected early 2027.
- Disc golf: **explicitly listed** in eligible activity categories ("outdoor adventure course activities: outdoor challenge course, **disc golf** or ropes course")
- Funded disc golf projects: "The Arena" in West Jordan ($200K, partnered with ElevateUT); Logan Outdoor Recreation Complex ($750K, includes 18-hole disc golf on 194 acres); Green River and Carbon County projects discussed in 2025 scoring meetings
- BLM-land applicability: federal agencies are listed as eligible applicants; nonprofits can apply for projects on BLM land with land manager authorization
- URL: https://recreation.utah.gov/grants/utah-outdoor-recreation-grant/

**Community Parks and Recreation Grant (CPR)**
- Range: **$5,000–$200,000**
- Purpose: rehabilitation and construction of community park assets
- 501(c)(3): yes
- URL: https://recreation.utah.gov/cpr-grant/

**Recreation Restoration Infrastructure Grant (RRI)**
- Range: up to **$250,000**
- Purpose: restore, repair, or replace aging outdoor recreation infrastructure on public lands
- 501(c)(3): yes
- Strategic note: ideal for upgrading informal BLM courses (e.g., the Sheep Mountain pattern in MT) to permanent facilities
- URL: https://recreation.utah.gov/grants/recreation-restoration-infrastructure-grant/

**Recreational Trails Program (RTP) — Utah**
- Range: **$10,000–$200,000**; 20% match
- 501(c)(3): yes
- 2026 cycle: February 15–April 1, 2026
- URL: https://recreation.utah.gov/grants/recreational-trails-program/

**Land and Water Conservation Fund (LWCF) — Utah**
- ~$2.8M general LWCF + ~$1M GOMESA annually; **$55M+** distributed since inception across 500+ projects
- 50% match
- 501(c)(3): **no** — political subdivisions and sovereign Indian Nations only
- URL: https://recreation.utah.gov/grants/land-water-conservation-fund/

**LeRay McAllister Critical Land Conservation Fund** — focused on land conservation, not recreation infrastructure. Low relevance.

**Notes:** Utah does not have a voter-approved dedicated recreation fund like Colorado's GOCO; UORG is funded through direct legislative appropriation. Many Utah communities also levy a **RAP Tax** (Recreation, Arts, and Parks, 0.1% local sales tax) — a useful municipal-level match source.

### NEW MEXICO — proven disc golf funding

**Outdoor Recreation Trails+ Grant Program**
- Agency: NM Outdoor Recreation Division (ORD), under the Economic Development Department
- Funding source: state general fund + Land of Enchantment Legacy Fund (recurring)
- Range: Tier 1 up to **$100,000**; Tier 2 $100,001–**$500,000**; Tier 3 up to $1,000,000 (Tier 3 limited to government entities)
- Match: in-kind and cash accepted; encouraged but not strict percentage
- 501(c)(3): **Tier 1 and Tier 2 eligible** ("nonprofits with capacity are now eligible")
- Annual program: ~**$10M+** annually; **$39.8M** distributed since 2020 to 282 projects across 29 counties
- Cycle: FY2026 closed January 31, 2026; **FY2027 opens July 1, 2026**
- Disc golf projects funded:
  - City of Albuquerque: **$130K** (FY24) — new 20-hole course at Puerto del Sol, rehab of Brent Baca Memorial DGC, new 12-hole at Westgate Park
  - Glorieta Adventure Camps: **$500K** (FY25) — 18-hole course plus trail system, parking, composting toilets, signage
  - Jemez Enterprises, Rio Arriba County: ~**$240K** — adventure park featuring disc golf course
- BLM-land precedent: Public Lands Interpretive Association received $80,650 for a project on BLM-managed land (Fort Craig). BLM evaluators have served as expert panelists for Trails+ reviews.
- URL: https://nmoutside.com/grants/trails-plus/

**Land of Enchantment Legacy Fund** — established 2023 (SB 9). Permanent fund balance **$382.9M** as of May 2025. Not a direct grant; provides recurring funding to Trails+ and Outdoor Equity Fund.

**LWCF — New Mexico** — EMNRD State Parks Division. ~$1.5M annually; 50% match; political subdivisions only. 2026 cycle opens September 2026. https://www.emnrd.nm.gov/spd/land-and-water-conservation-fund/

**RTP — New Mexico** — EMNRD; up to $200K; 20% match. 501(c)(3) eligibility unclear — verify with EMNRD.

**Severance Tax Bonds** — Legislature annually authorizes capital project bonds (Series 2025A: $237.8M). Access is political, not competitive — requires legislative champion.

### COLORADO — large funding, but nonprofits need a government partner

**Great Outdoors Colorado (GOCO)**
- Independent entity created by 1992 constitutional amendment; funded by Colorado Lottery (FY24: **$81.7M**)
- Community Impact Program: **$100,000–$2,000,000** per project (starting FY27, projects above $2M considered for 1–2 per year)
- Match: customized, historically 25–50%
- 501(c)(3): **not for general nonprofits.** Eligible: CO municipalities, counties, Title 32 special districts, nonprofit *land conservation* organizations, political subdivisions with open space missions, CPW. A disc golf nonprofit must partner with a local government applicant.
- Cycles: biannual — concept papers ~January and ~July; awards ~June and ~December
- Disc golf precedent: Las Animas Recreation HUB ($750K, included disc golf upgrades); Delta Fairgrounds ($598,843, included "frisbee golf course"); Fountain Regional Park 18-hole course; Alamosa area project
- Total invested since 1992: **$1.4B+** across 5,800+ projects in all 64 counties
- URL: https://goco.org/programs-projects/our-grant-programs

Other GOCO programs: Pathways (planning/capacity), Land Acquisition (up to $3M), Conservation Service Corps (~$1M/year via Colorado Youth Corps Association), Regional Partnerships Initiative ($50M, 2025–2030, CPW manages), RESTORE Colorado ($4.5M for watershed restoration — nonprofits eligible but not for recreation infrastructure).

**Colorado Parks and Wildlife Grants**
- LWCF — Colorado: $100K–$1.25M; 50% match; government agencies only. https://cpw.state.co.us/lwcf
- Non-Motorized Trails Grant: up to $250K construction, $250K maintenance, $45K planning. Some nonprofit trail orgs have received grants (Crested Butte Mountain Bike Association, Headwaters Trails Alliance). https://cpw.state.co.us/grant-programs
- Outdoor Equity Grant: up to $4M per cycle; nonprofits eligible; **programming only**, not capital infrastructure (HB25-1215 strengthened in 2025).

**Conservation Trust Fund (CTF)** — automatic quarterly per-capita distribution of 40% of lottery proceeds to 480+ eligible local governments (~$65M+ annually). Not competitive. **Strategy:** approach municipalities about dedicating CTF allocation to a disc golf project or using it as match for GOCO. https://dlg.colorado.gov/conservation-trust-fund-ctf

**Colorado Office of Outdoor Recreation Industry (OREC)** — no permanent state-funded grant program. Prior grants (COSORG, ORIIF) were federal pandemic recovery funds.

### WASHINGTON — deepest portfolio, mostly through RCO

The Washington Recreation and Conservation Office (RCO) administers dozens of programs with combined biennial funding above $120M.

**Washington Wildlife and Recreation Program (WWRP) — Recreation Categories**
- 2025 biennial funding: **$120M** (all categories combined)
- Categories: Local Parks, Trails, Water Access, State Parks, State Lands Development & Renovation
- Max: **$1,000,000** per project; 50% match (≥10% from non-state, non-federal)
- 501(c)(3): **no** — cities, counties, special purpose districts, state agencies, tribes only
- Deadline: April 30, 2026
- URL: https://rco.wa.gov/grant/washington-wildlife-and-recreation-program-recreation/

**Youth Athletic Facilities (YAF)**
- Max: **$1,500,000**; 50% match
- 501(c)(3): **directly eligible**
- Restriction: jurisdiction with ≤10,000 residents (cities/towns) or county <60,000 (unincorporated)
- Disc golf qualifies as outdoor athletic facility
- Deadline: April 30, 2026
- URL: https://rco.wa.gov/grant/youth-athletic-facilities/

**Planning for Recreation Access (PRA)**
- Match: **none required**
- 501(c)(3): yes
- Purpose: planning/pre-design for recreation facilities in underserved communities
- Deadline: ~July 1, 2026 (webinar May 13, 2026)
- Strategic value: gateway grant to plan a course before applying for larger construction funding
- URL: https://rco.wa.gov/grant/plan-rec-access/

**Nonhighway and Off-Road Vehicle Activities (NOVA)**
- $150K maintenance / $200K development; no match required
- 501(c)(3): yes — must be authorized to develop recreational facilities
- Restriction: must be accessed via non-highway road (BLM, NF, state forest roads)
- Deadline: November 3, 2026
- URL: https://rco.wa.gov/grant/nonhighway-and-off-road-vehicle-activities-program-trails/

**RTP — Washington** — RCO; max $150K; 20% match; nonprofits eligible. Deadline November 3, 2026.

**LWCF — Washington** — RCO; nonprofits not eligible. Next cycle February 2028.

**Washington Office of Outdoor Recreation** — no direct grants; coordination/policy office inside RCO.

### OREGON — strong programs but nonprofit access is limited

**OPRD Local Government Grant Program (LGGP)**
- Funding: Oregon State Lottery (voter-approved); **$15M+** annually; $100M+ since 1999
- Range: Large up to **$1,000,000**; Small up to $100K; Small Community Planning up to $50K
- Match: 40–50% (reduced for communities under 5,000)
- 501(c)(3): **no** — local government agencies only
- 2026 deadlines: Large June 5, 2026; Small/Planning June 18, 2026
- URL: https://www.oregon.gov/oprd/GRA/pages/GRA-lggp.aspx

**RTP — Oregon**
- Range: $10K minimum; recommended max **$200K** (non-motorized)
- Match: 20% (volunteer labor and in-kind accepted)
- 501(c)(3): **directly eligible** — most accessible OPRD program for nonprofits
- Next cycle: Fall 2026 (criteria being updated per 2026 Oregon Recreational Trails Plan)
- URL: https://www.oregon.gov/oprd/GRA/Pages/GRA-rtp.aspx

**LWCF — Oregon** — ~$4M annually; 50% match; nonprofits not eligible.

**Oregon Conservation & Recreation Fund (OCRF)** — ODFW; max $25K; nonprofits eligible. Best for educational/interpretive components emphasizing wildlife habitat. Two cycles per year. https://www.dfw.state.or.us/conservationstrategy/OCRF/grants.html

**Travel Oregon Competitive Grants** — $20K–$150K (competitive); $500K+ (Large Scale Investment); nonprofits eligible. 2026 LOI April–May. https://industry.traveloregon.com/grants/competitive-grants-program/

**BLM Secure Rural Schools Title II Grants (Oregon)** — nonprofits eligible; 18 western Oregon counties with O&C lands; no match. **Disc golf precedent: Stewart Pond DGC** near Roseburg, OR — established disc golf course on BLM land with official BLM support.

### CALIFORNIA — massive bond funding with nonprofit access

**Statewide Park Development and Community Revitalization Program (SPP)**
- Agency: California State Parks, OGALS
- Funding: Proposition 68 (2018) and now **Proposition 4 (2024 Climate Bond, $200M for SPP)**
- Range: up to ~$5–8.5M per project historically
- Match: none required for Prop 68 rounds; check Prop 4 Round 5 guidelines
- 501(c)(3): **directly eligible**
- Restriction: underserved communities (MHI under ~$77K or fewer than 3 park acres per 1,000 residents)
- Next cycle: **Round 5 opening Summer/Fall 2026** (Prop 4 funded)
- URL: https://www.parks.ca.gov/?page_id=29939

**Proposition 4 (2024 Climate Bond)** — passed November 5, 2024 (58%); $10B total; **$700M for parks/recreation** ($200M SPP; $175M deferred maintenance; $119M outdoor recreation/climate for disadvantaged communities). $466M of $700M allocated in first year. Eligibility: "Grants and loans to local governments, Native American tribes, **not-for-profit organizations**, and businesses."

**RTP — California (Non-Motorized)** — OGALS; ~$6–8M annually statewide; 12–20% match; nonprofits eligible. https://www.parks.ca.gov/?page_id=24324

**LWCF — California** — $20–40M+ annually; public agencies only.

**Per Capita Grant Program** — non-competitive block grant to qualifying local agencies; nonprofits not eligible (partner with local agency).

**Outdoor Equity Grants Program** — defunct/unfunded. Round 2 canceled due to budget deficit.

**California Office of Outdoor Recreation (CalOutdoors)** — no direct grants.

### NEVADA — Conserve Nevada and SNPLMA are primary pathways

**Conserve Nevada Program**
- Agency: Nevada Department of Conservation and Natural Resources (NDCNR)
- Funding: state general obligation bonds — AB 84 (2019) authorized **$217.5M over 10 years**, renewing original Question 1 (2002) voter-approved $200M bond
- Current round: ~**$10M** available
- 501(c)(3): **directly eligible**
- Eligible projects: explicitly includes "design/construction of recreation facilities, campsites, or trails"
- Deadline: March 6, 2026 (2026 round; next TBD)
- 200+ projects funded across every county; 100+ miles of trails
- URL: https://dcnr.nv.gov/divisions-boards/conserve-nevada

**Southern Nevada Public Land Management Act (SNPLMA)**
- Agency: BLM Southern Nevada District
- Funding: revenue from sale of BLM land in Las Vegas Valley (**$4.5B+** generated since 1998)
- Most recent round: Round 20 — **$375M** for 36 projects (2024)
- Parks, Trails, and Natural Areas (PTNA) is the disc golf-relevant category
- 501(c)(3): **no** — local government entities only
- Geographic limit: 5 NV counties (Carson City, Clark, Lincoln, White Pine, Churchill)
- Disc golf precedent on BLM land: **Ward Mountain Recreation Area, Ely, NV** — 18-hole course on BLM land in White Pine County (SNPLMA-eligible). Paver tee pads, rated 4.1/5 on UDisc. BLM produced official course map through Avenza Maps.
- URL: https://www.blm.gov/programs/lands-and-realty/regional-information/nevada/snplma

**RTP — Nevada** — Nevada Division of State Parks; ~$1.2M annually; max $200K; nonprofits eligible. 2026 awards already made; check 2027 cycle.

**LWCF — Nevada** — political subdivisions only.

**Nevada Outdoor Education and Recreation Grant (NOER)** — Nevada Division of Outdoor Recreation (NDOR); $250K annually; Tier I up to $7,500; Tier II up to $50,000; nonprofits eligible. **Youth education programs only — not capital infrastructure.** https://ndor.nv.gov/noer-grants

### MONTANA — Trail Stewardship is the standout state-funded program

**Montana Trail Stewardship Grant Program (TSP)**
- Agency: Montana Fish, Wildlife & Parks (FWP)
- Funding: $1.37 from each $9 vehicle registration fee + recreational marijuana tax revenue (HB 701, 2021)
- Annual: ~**$2M** per cycle; **$7.6M** to 180+ projects since inception
- Max: **$100,000**
- Match: **10%** of total project cost — the **lowest of any program in this inventory**
- 501(c)(3): **directly eligible** ("Tribal, state, county, municipal, or federal agencies, school districts, and nonprofit recreational organizations and clubs")
- Cycle: December 1–January 30 annually
- Eligible: new trail/path construction, rehabilitation, trailside facilities (bridges, fencing, parking, restrooms, signage, weed management)
- **Stackable with RTP** — TSP explicitly permits using other FWP grants as match
- ⚠️ Risk: 2025 Montana Legislature may attempt to reallocate marijuana tax revenue
- URL: https://fwp.mt.gov/aboutfwp/grant-programs/trail-stewardship

**RTP — Montana** — FWP; ~$1.5M annual program; max **$100K**; 20% match; nonprofits eligible. Deadline November 1–January 15. https://fwp.mt.gov/aboutfwp/grant-programs/recreational-trails

**LWCF — Montana** — ~$1.71M annually; 50% match; government entities only. Next cycle Summer 2027.

**Montana Tourism Grant Program** — Department of Commerce; 4% lodging tax; 50% match; nonprofits eligible. Best for signage, mapping, destination components.

**Montana Office of Outdoor Recreation** — does not exist as a grant-making office. Montana Access Project (mtaccessproject.com) provides technical assistance only.

**BLM disc golf precedent in MT:** Sheep Mountain disc golf courses near Clancy — 18-hole main + "Hidden Course" on BLM land. Currently informal (gong-style targets, informal tee pads). Public comment campaign underway for official BLM designation.

## The Recreational Trails Program: cross-cutting status

The RTP is the single most important cross-cutting program for a 501(c)(3) disc golf organization — the one federal pass-through that consistently allows direct nonprofit applicants in nearly every state.

- **Current authorization:** through federal **FY2026** under the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA/BIL), as a set-aside from Transportation Alternatives
- **National funding:** ~**$84M** annually (each state's allocation equals its FY2009 apportionment)
- **⚠️ Reauthorization risk:** authorization expires after FY2026. Congress must include RTP in the next surface transportation bill. S.811 "RTP Full Funding Act of 2025" proposes increasing funding to ~$281M (matching actual nonhighway fuel tax collections) but has not passed.
- **Federal budget context:** the Trump Administration's FY2027 budget proposes 31% cuts to Forest Service "Recreation, Heritage and Wilderness" management and 64% cuts to trail maintenance. Congress rejected similar FY2026 proposals. RTP funding itself comes from the Highway Trust Fund, not agency appropriations, providing some insulation — but political risk is real.
- **Match:** universally 20% (volunteer labor and in-kind accepted in most states)

**Strategic implication:** RTP awards should be filed promptly while the program is still authorized, and they should be used as match-stacking for larger state programs wherever co-stacking is allowed (Montana TSP+RTP is the cleanest example).

## Strategic sequencing

For a new national 501(c)(3) building its first disc golf courses on BLM land, the optimal sequence is:

1. **Secure BLM land authorization first** — Special Recreation Permit, cooperative agreement, or other authority described in [07-blm-engagement-package.md](07-blm-engagement-package.md). State grants need a defensible site and a willing land manager before they will score well.
2. **File with directly nonprofit-eligible state programs** — UORG, Trails+, Conserve Nevada, RTPs in Utah, Oregon, Idaho, Arizona, Alaska, Montana, Washington. These do not require political waiting.
3. **Cultivate local government partnerships in parallel** to unlock the larger programs that require government applicants — GOCO, WWRP, LGGP, LWCF (in every state), SNPLMA, Wyoming BRC. The county/municipal partner becomes the applicant of record; the disc golf nonprofit provides volunteer capacity, design, and operational continuity.
4. **Stack foundation grants and small RTP awards as match** for the larger asks. Montana's TSP-plus-RTP combination — two small grants with 10% and 20% match respectively — is the cleanest template; replicate the pattern in any state where dual programs exist.
5. **Sequence cycles** — most state programs run on annual or biennial cycles that don't overlap. Build a calendar by January, lining up which state program each in-flight site will hit in which window.
