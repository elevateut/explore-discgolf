# Sources

## Primary statute

- **EXPLORE Act (Public Law 118-234)**, 138 Stat. 2836, signed January 4, 2025. Codified principally at 16 U.S.C. Chapter 103. Full text available via Congress.gov: H.R. 6492, 118th Congress.
- **H. Rept. 118-441** — House Natural Resources Committee report on H.R. 6492 (April 5, 2024).

## Congressional record and legislative history

- H.R. 6492 introduced November 29, 2023, by Rep. Bruce Westerman (R-AR) with Rep. Raul Grijalva (D-AZ). 51 bipartisan cosponsors.
- Senate companion: America's Outdoor Recreation Act, led by Sen. Joe Manchin (D-WV) and Sen. John Barrasso (R-WY).
- House passed by voice vote, April 9, 2024.
- Senate passed by unanimous consent, December 19, 2024.
- House Natural Resources Subcommittee on Federal Lands hearing: "EXPLORE America250 — Celebrating One Year of the EXPLORE Act," January 21, 2026. Witness: Thomas Heinlein, Acting Utah State Director, BLM.

## Disc golf industry and participation data

- **UDisc 2026 Growth Report** — 11,165 U.S. courses; 21.2 million rounds logged in 2025; 32.8 million player-hours; 1,650 new courses 2022-2024; 89% of courses free; 3 new courses per day in 2025; 121 average play days per year; 93-minute average round; 3,800 steps; 400 calories per round; $425 million global market.
- **Consumer surplus study** — Peer-reviewed travel cost model at FDR/Heckscher State Parks, NY. Individual consumer surplus ~$68 per trip; ~$6 million aggregate annual surplus for single urban-adjacent course.
- **USDGC/CDGNC economic impact** — Two events in York County, SC generated $4.5 million in economic activity.
- **Creekside DGC, Salt Lake City, UT** — 59,142 recreation-hours on ~$200,000 investment. (Scott's data/ElevateUT)
- **Dragonfly DGC** — 11,824 recreation-hours with near-zero city funding. (Scott's data/ElevateUT)
- **Cost comparisons** — 9-hole disc golf course: $9,000-$18,000. Single pickleball court: $28,000-$37,500. 18-hole volunteer build: $15,000-$25,000. Professional install: $30,000-$50,000+.
- **Dunkin' Donuts U.S. locations** — 10,014 (comparison benchmark for 11,165 disc golf courses).
- **Environmental behavior study** — 97% of disc golfers willing to modify behavior to offset environmental impacts (peer-reviewed).

## Federal disc golf precedents

- **Three Peaks and Ironside DGC, Cedar City, UT** — BLM Cedar City Field Office. Partnership with Southern Utah University Disc Golf Club. Two 18-hole courses. Innova DISCatcher baskets, concrete tee pads, dual pin placements. Ironside: 6,761-7,444 feet elevation.
- **Stewart Pond DGC, Eugene, OR** — BLM Northwest Oregon District. Partnership with Eugene Disc Golf Club. 18-hole, scalable to 9/12/18. "Hundreds of hours of donated volunteer time." Opened September 2016.
- **Ward Mountain, Ely, NV** — BLM Bristlecone Field Office. 14-hole course.
- **Barnes Grade Recreation Area** — BLM Applegate Field Office. 9-hole course approved September 2025.
- **Shady Rest DiscGolfPark, Mammoth Lakes, CA** — Inyo National Forest (USFS). 20-month approval process (January 2018 to September 2019). Zero trees removed. Designed by Avery Jenkins (2009 World Champion). Town of Mammoth Lakes Parks & Rec managed USFS relationship.
- **Army Corps of Engineers courses** — Black Butte Lake, CA; Sugarbottom, Turkey Creek, Walnut Ridge, and Big Creek in Iowa.
- **Bear Mountain DGC, CO** — Designed as wildfire prevention infrastructure (half-mile defensible fire line).

## Organizational models

- **IMBA (International Mountain Bicycling Association)** — Founded 1988, Boulder, CO. 501(c)(3). ~$7 million annual revenue. $20 million "Leading with Trails" capital campaign. ~248 chapters via IMBA Local program ($15/member/year, $200 setup). First USFS MOU 1994. BLM cooperative agreement since 2000. National Trail Classification System development 2006. Founding member of Outdoor Alliance. EXPLORE Act Section 121 (BOLT Act) resulted from IMBA advocacy.
- **Access Fund** — Founded 1991, Boulder, CO. ~$5-7 million annually. 150+ local climbing organizations. Climbing Conservation Grant Program ($1,000-$4,000). EXPLORE Act Section 122 (PARC Act) codifying climbing in wilderness — Access Fund's crowning legislative achievement. $12 billion climbing industry data.
- **Trout Unlimited** — Founded 1959. ~400 chapters, ~300,000 members. ~$50-100 million annual budget. $8.9 million in BLM partnership agreements. Revenue grew from $2 million (1992) to ~$100 million primarily through federal grant leverage.
- **Backcountry Hunters & Anglers** — Founded 2004. 7 founders to 40,000+ members across 48 states. Nonpartisan identity: 1/3 D, 1/3 R, 1/3 I.
- **Outdoor Alliance** — 10 member organizations, 250,000+ advocates. 323 meetings with lawmakers in 2025. Grasstops Collective Congressional fly-in.
- **Outdoor Recreation Roundtable** — 80+ members. Led EXPLORE Act to passage. $1.3 trillion outdoor recreation economy, 5.2 million jobs. President Jessica Wahl Turner met with Secretary Burgum, September 2025.

## PDGA and Disc Golf Foundation

- **PDGA** — Founded 1976, Appling, Georgia. 501(c)(4) social welfare organization. Sport governance: rules, ratings, tournament sanctioning. International Disc Golf Center on 975 acres. Environmental Committee: Throw Green Initiative.
- **Disc Golf Foundation** — Separate 501(c)(3). Matching Baskets Program for college courses; 17 campus courses installed.

## Tax and nonprofit law

- **IRS guidance on 501(c)(3)** — Can receive tax-deductible contributions; lobbying cannot be substantial part of activities. Expenditure test via Form 5768: orgs under $500K can spend up to 20% on lobbying.
- **IRS guidance on 501(c)(4)** — Social welfare organization may lobby as primary activity.
- **IRS Form 1023** — Application for 501(c)(3) status. Processing: 3-6 months typical. Filing fee: $600. 27-month retroactive window from incorporation.
