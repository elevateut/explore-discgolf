# Current State: Disc Golf on Federal Lands and EXPLORE Act Implementation

## Disc golf is already a recognized BLM recreation use

This is an important starting point that changes the advocacy framing. **BLM's public "Visit" system already includes DISC GOLF as an activity category**, and BLM Utah/Cedar City pages currently promote disc golf at Three Peaks Recreation Area, including separate BLM pages for the Three Peaks and Ironside 18-hole courses. A disc golf proposal starts from an activity the agency already recognizes on its own lands, not from a brand-new concept.

## Existing BLM disc golf courses

### Stewart Pond Disc Golf Course, Eugene, Oregon
**BLM Northwest Oregon District.** BLM's first 18-hole disc golf course in Oregon/Washington, opened September 2016 in the West Eugene Wetlands. Built through multi-year collaboration between the **Eugene Disc Golf Club** and BLM, with "hundreds of hours of donated volunteer time." Features concrete tee pads, dual basket positions, scalable layout (9, 12, or 18 holes depending on seasonal conditions), and a kiosk with course map. Free, open dawn to dusk. BLM actively manages this site: in July 2025 BLM implemented a 4-hour parking limit to ensure equitable access among disc golf, hiking, and birdwatching users. In September 2025, BLM hosted a volunteer service project to maintain the course.

### Three Peaks and Ironside Disc Golf Courses, Cedar City, Utah
**BLM Cedar City Field Office.** Two 18-hole courses in the Three Peaks Recreation Area, built through partnership between **Southern Utah University Disc Golf Club** and BLM-Utah. Features large concrete tee pads, Innova DISCatcher baskets, course map signage at every tee pad, dual pin placements, gravel parking, and informational kiosk. High desert terrain with granite formations and pinion/juniper. Ironside plays at 6,761-7,444 feet. Free, with a donation box on site.

### Ward Mountain Recreation Area, Ely, Nevada
**BLM Bristlecone Field Office.** A 14-hole course. Limited public documentation available.

## Additional federal disc golf precedent

**Shady Rest DiscGolfPark, Mammoth Lakes, California (Inyo National Forest).** Required a 20-month approval process (January 2018 to September 2019). Environmental surveys were required; zero trees were removed. Designed by 2009 World Champion Avery Jenkins. The Town of Mammoth Lakes Parks & Recreation managed the USFS relationship while the Mammoth Lakes Disc Golf Club provided community support and fundraising.

Additional courses exist on Army Corps of Engineers land (Black Butte Lake, CA; Sugarbottom, Turkey Creek, Walnut Ridge, and Big Creek in Iowa) and on USFS leased ski resort land.

## Post-Act field office disc golf activity

### Barnes Grade Recreation Area (Applegate Field Office)
In September 2025, the Applegate Field Office issued a decision authorizing the Barnes Grade Recreation Area, including three parking areas, one day-use area, **a nine-hole disc golf course**, and related infrastructure. This is hard evidence that a BLM field office approved disc golf infrastructure after the Act became law.

**Important caveat:** No public BLM document explicitly says Barnes Grade was approved *because of* the EXPLORE Act. The fair statement: it is evidence that disc golf is very much within the kind of recreation development BLM is advancing in the EXPLORE era, not proof that a specific EXPLORE section directly triggered that course.

## EXPLORE Act implementation as of April 2026

### Secretary's Order 3435
Issued June 25, 2025 by Secretary Doug Burgum. Established DOI's framework for implementing the EXPLORE Act, ensuring "strong internal management and timely compliance, meaningful stakeholder engagement and private sector coordination."

### Direct implementation milestones
- **Digital America the Beautiful passes** launched January 1, 2026 via Recreation.gov (Section 322)
- **RAPTOR system Phase I** went live February 2, 2026, enabling electronic SRP applications under Title III reforms
- **New categorical exclusions adopted** for SRP processing (per DOI testimony, January 2026)
- **BLM completed nationwide shooting range inventory** per Section 123 (September 2025)
- **Good Neighbor Authority recreation guidance** under development (Section 351)
- **Accessible recreation opportunities** identification underway across BLM, NPS, FWS lands (Sections 214-215)
- **Thomas Heinlein**, Acting Utah State Director for BLM, testified before the House Natural Resources Subcommittee on January 21, 2026, at a hearing titled "EXPLORE America250 — Celebrating One Year of the EXPLORE Act"

### Early investment and staffing
- BLM collaborated with the Foundation for America's Public Lands on **10 recreation and access projects** in Arizona, California, Colorado, Florida, Montana, New Mexico, Utah, and Wyoming, totaling **$1 million in direct investments and $4 million in matching funds**
- BLM expanded staff, formed a dedicated EXPLORE "core team," and began developing field-office guidance
- FY2026 budget justification explicitly says BLM is implementing Title III permitting improvements
- BLM recorded **more than 80 million recreation visits** in FY2024

### Accessibility rollout (strongest early momentum)
- BLM added enhanced accessibility descriptions to **more than 100 recreation sites** and assembled photos for **76+ sites**
- Second phase started in Utah, updating nearly 25 Utah Visit pages
- State-by-state accessibility stories published, tied explicitly to supporting the EXPLORE Act
- Highlights: Phoenix District's accessible shooting sites, Colorado's Uncompahgre Field Office adaptive bike trail, Idaho's adaptive-ready Hang Tight/Hang Loose trail

### Field-office level reporting
- Northern California RAC minutes (May 2025): BLM examining each EXPLORE Act subsection, identifying policy/regulatory changes, developing implementation guidance and timelines
- Applegate: increased work developing recreation trails
- Eagle Lake: recreation accomplishments reported
- Redding: campground openings, recreation accomplishments, planning, and outreach

### Implementation challenges
Winter Wildlands Alliance noted in January 2026 that "implementation is contingent on having land management agencies like the Forest Service, National Park Service, and BLM staffed and funded." Ongoing federal workforce reductions have slowed timelines. BLM acknowledged "unanticipated challenges or growing pains" with new systems.

## Bottom line

The strongest documented EXPLORE Act use so far is **system and policy implementation** (permits, accessibility, data, passes), while the strongest disc-golf-specific evidence is that **BLM already manages disc golf, publicly categorizes it as a recreation use, and has at least one recent field-office disc golf development decision on the books.**
